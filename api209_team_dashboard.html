API-209 Teaching Team — Task Map

When to use Chat vs. Cowork across the semester

Chat — think together
Cowork — execute a task
Either works
Before the semester
Chat Create the Teaching Team Guide for a new course
Adapt the API-209 template — update people, dates, schedules, links. Chat is useful for wordsmithing and discussing what to keep or cut.
Before week 1
Cowork Set up the Course Management Sheet for the new semester
Duplicate the Google Sheet, clear last year's entries, update rosters, and verify all tab links and formulas.
Before week 1
Cowork Onboard TF and CAs with personalized role sheets
Generate individual onboarding documents from the guide — one per role — with their specific responsibilities and schedules highlighted.
Before week 1
Chat Review and update the syllabus or PS schedule
Talk through pacing, difficulty curve, and any changes from last year's "improving next year" notes.
Before week 1
Weekly — grading cycle
Cowork Monitor submission status and flag non-submissions
Check Canvas roster against submissions, generate a non-submission list, confirm grades are muted.
PS due date + 1 day
Cowork Compile CA grading reports into the common mistakes table
Read CA notes from Drive, extract top errors per question, write them into the Teaching Team Guide table for that PS.
After grading closes
Chat Handle a student grading dispute or struggling student
Talk through the situation, draft a response, or think through how to intervene constructively.
As needed
Either Review a problem set for clarity and difficulty
Chat for judgment calls on question design; Cowork to format the PS document or generate a solution key draft.
Before PS release
Weekly — in-class operations
Cowork Check class observation sheet for gaps or missing entries
Read the Google Sheet, flag if any week's Teachly, observations, or Slack columns are blank, notify the responsible CA.
After each class
Chat Draft student announcements
Weekly emails, Canvas announcements, PS reminders, office hour notices — Chat matches tone and course context well.
Each week
Either Prepare Slack discussion questions or prompts
Chat for brainstorming with you; Cowork to batch-generate a week's worth of prompts from the syllabus topics.
Each week
Chat Weekly team check-in prep
Synthesize signals from class observations, grading reports, and Slack to surface what needs attention this week.
Each week
Exam weeks
Chat Review exam draft
Check question clarity, solution logic, and difficulty curve. Requires real judgment and understanding of the course material.
Week before exam
Cowork Generate proctor and processor role sheets
Read the exam logistics tables from the guide, produce individual role checklists for each team member for exam day.
Week before exam
Cowork Compile common mistakes table after grading
Read grader notes from Gradescope exports, summarize by question number, populate the midterm/final common mistakes table.
Day after exam
Cowork Collect and organize exam day lunch orders
Read form responses, build the lunch order table in the guide's format, flag any missing preferences.
Week before exam
End of semester
Chat Synthesize "improving next year" notes into a plan
Discuss what mattered, what to prioritize, and how to turn raw notes into actionable changes for the guide.
After finals
Cowork Update the Teaching Team Guide with this year's notes
Read all end-of-semester inputs, write them into the correct guide sections, rename files to next year, clear operational tables.
After finals
Chat Draft CA recommendations for next year
Talk through which students stood out and why, then draft the recommendation section in the guide's format.
After finals
Cowork Archive this year's files and reset the sheet for next year
Move graded PSets, exam files, and CA reports to an archive folder; reset the management sheet for next semester.
